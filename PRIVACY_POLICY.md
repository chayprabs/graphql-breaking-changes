# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** May 31, 2026  
**Last updated:** May 31, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how **GraphQLGuard** (the "Service"), made available at the GraphQLGuard website and related open-source repositories (collectively, the "Site"), handles information when you use the Service.

The **Operator** of the Service is Chaitanya Prabuddha ("we", "us", "our"). For contact details, see Section 12.

By accessing or using the Service, you acknowledge this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the Service.

---

## 1. Summary

GraphQLGuard is designed as a **browser-only** application. Schema text, introspection JSON, operation files, and diff results are intended to be processed **on your device** in your web browser. We do not operate an application backend that receives, stores, or analyzes your GraphQL content for the core playground workflow.

---

## 2. Information we do not collect

To the extent we control the hosted Site, we **do not intentionally collect, purchase, or sell**:

- GraphQL SDL, introspection JSON, or operation file contents you paste or upload;
- Filenames or paths of files you select locally;
- Diff, lint, federation, or coverage results generated from your inputs;
- Account credentials (the Service does not require login).

We do not use third-party analytics, advertising pixels, or session replay tools that are designed to exfiltrate your schema content or file names.

---

## 3. Local storage on your device

The Service may store editor text and preferences in your browser's **local storage** (or similar browser technologies) so you can continue work on the same device. This data remains on your device unless you clear it.

You can remove locally stored data at any time using the **"Clear saved data"** control in the playground, or by clearing site data in your browser settings.

We do not have access to your local storage unless you voluntarily send it to us (for example, by opening a GitHub issue and pasting content).

---

## 4. Information that may be processed automatically (hosting)

If you visit a **hosted** copy of the Site, standard web hosting and content delivery may automatically process limited technical data, such as:

- IP address;
- Browser type and version;
- Request timestamps and URLs;
- Referrer and basic device/network metadata needed to deliver static files.

This processing is typically performed by **infrastructure providers** (for example, a static host or CDN), not for profiling you or reading your schema inputs. Exact logs depend on your host's configuration if you self-host.

We do not use this technical data to reconstruct your GraphQL schemas.

If you interact with our public GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions), GitHub's privacy policy applies to that activity. **Do not post production secrets, customer data, or confidential schemas** in public issues.
